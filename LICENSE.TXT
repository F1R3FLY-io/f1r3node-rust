# Sovereign Source License (SSL) v0.3

## Preamble

The Sovereign Source License (SSL) extends the Apache License 2.0 to create a sustainable ecosystem for open source development through intelligent data collection and monetization. This license preserves all Apache 2.0 rights and freedoms while adding optional enhanced services that fund continued development through the collection, curation, and sale of development-related data.

Like Apache 2.0, SSL enables unrestricted use, modification, and distribution. Unlike traditional licenses that provide no compensation mechanism, SSL adds an optional ecosystem layer that creates value from the development process itself, benefiting contributors while maintaining complete compatibility with Apache 2.0 usage patterns.

## Definitions

**Note**: This license incorporates and extends all definitions from the Apache License 2.0. The following definitions are additions or clarifications to the Apache 2.0 framework.

1. **Work**: As defined in Apache 2.0 - the work of authorship made available under the License, whether in Source or Object form.

2. **Contribution**: As defined in Apache 2.0 - any work of authorship intentionally submitted for inclusion in the Work.

3. **Contributor**: As defined in Apache 2.0 - Licensor and any individual or Legal Entity on behalf of whom a Contribution has been received by Licensor and subsequently incorporated within the Work.

4. **Smart-Assets.io Ecosystem** *(SSL Extension)*: The optional technical and economic framework consisting of:
   - SSL Data Spigot for LLM interaction routing and data collection
   - SATCHEL cross-chain wallet system for payments and credits
   - Semantic Git repositories for enhanced source code representation
   - Data marketplace for selling curated development datasets

5. **Development Data** *(SSL Extension)*: Information generated through optional ecosystem development activities including:
   - LLM prompts and responses during coding assistance
   - Semantic source code representations and analysis
   - Code contribution patterns and usage metrics
   - Structured specifications and documentation traces

6. **SSL Data Spigot** *(SSL Extension)*: The optional system that routes LLM backend access while collecting, formatting, and curating development data for the ecosystem marketplace.

7. **SATCHEL** *(SSL Extension)*: The optional cross-chain smart contract wallet with integrated payment gateway supporting cryptocurrency transactions and ecosystem credit management.

8. **Structural Contributions** *(SSL Extension)*: Formalized domain knowledge contributed through interactive participation in ecosystem training and ontology development activities, including but not limited to: causal graphs (directed acyclic graphs specifying causal relationships), structural equations defining functional mechanisms, interventional experiment designs, counterfactual validations, and compressed programs expressing causal structure. Structural Contributions are distinct from both code contributions (covered under Apache 2.0 terms) and Development Data (definition 5). Their value is assessed dynamically by ecosystem value assessment mechanisms, such as BountyForge, based on the degree to which they compress the knowledge required to achieve equivalent predictive or generative capability.

9. **Causal Training Data** *(SSL Extension)*: Data generated through active participation in interactive training workflows that produces knowledge beyond observational association, including: interventional examples demonstrating the effects of targeted changes, counterfactual augmentations exploring alternative outcomes, refinements to causal models, and human-validated structural equations. Causal Training Data is categorically distinct from Development Data (definition 5) in that it encodes mechanistic relationships rather than statistical patterns.

10. **Ecosystem Credits** *(SSL Extension)*: Optional tokens earned through contributions and participation, redeemable for services, discounts, or direct compensation within the ecosystem.

**3.4. Structural Participation Tier (SSL Extension)**: Beyond basic ecosystem services, participants may engage in active co-creation of causal knowledge through interactive training, ontology specification, and structural contribution activities. Structural Participation is a distinct engagement tier characterized by:
   - Active contribution of Structural Contributions (definition 8) and Causal Training Data (definition 9) rather than passive data collection
   - Collaborative design of interventional experiments and counterfactual validations
   - Participation in causal ontology specification, refinement, and compression
   - Access to value-weighted compensation reflecting the higher data-efficiency of structural contributions relative to observational data
   - Dynamic value tracking through ecosystem value assessment mechanisms, such as BountyForge, that measure contribution impact by the degree of knowledge compression achieved

Structural Participation requires explicit agreement to structural participation terms in addition to standard ecosystem service terms. Structural Participants retain authorship of their Structural Contributions subject to ecosystem rights as defined in Section 6.

**3.5. Cross-Layer Data Flow Architecture (SSL Extension)**: The ecosystem processes distinct categories of data through specialized layers, each handling different types of contribution:
   - **Gateway layer**: Request routing and entry point management
   - **Trace collection layer** (e.g., SSL Data Spigot): LLM interaction traces, consent enforcement, and observational Development Data curation
   - **Semantic representation layer** (e.g., SSL Semantic Git): Vector embeddings, semantic code representations, and enhanced source code analysis
   - **Value assessment layer** (e.g., BountyForge): Quality metrics, contribution scoring, and compensation determination across all contribution types
   - **Causal distillation layer** (e.g., DaaS): Structural Contributions, Causal Training Data, interventional and counterfactual data generation, and causal model refinement

Each layer collects, processes, and generates data appropriate to its function. Consent, ownership, and compensation terms apply according to the data category (Development Data or Causal Training Data) and the participation tier (Ecosystem Services or Structural Participation) under which the data is contributed.

### 5. Developer Compensation and Sustainability (SSL Extension)

**5.1. Ecosystem Credits**: Contributors may earn optional credits through:
   - Code contributions and their measured impact
   - Data contribution quality and usage value
   - Ecosystem participation and community building
   - Curation and specification development activities

**5.2. Revenue Sharing Model**: Net proceeds from optional ecosystem data sales are distributed across four recipient categories: contributors, ecosystem infrastructure and development, data curation and quality assurance, and governance and dispute resolution. Distribution ratios are determined dynamically by ecosystem value assessment mechanisms based on contribution type and measured value, subject to the following principles:
   - Structural Contributions (definition 8) and Causal Training Data (definition 9) are weighted higher than observational Development Data (definition 5), reflecting the demonstrated data-efficiency of causal structure relative to statistical association -- structural contributions that enable equivalent capability from less data represent proportionally greater value
   - Contribution value is assessed using metrics that measure knowledge compression: the degree to which a contribution reduces the total description length required to achieve equivalent predictive or generative performance
   - Specific weightings and formulas are defined by ecosystem-compatible source code implementations rather than fixed percentages, allowing adaptation as valuation methodologies mature
   - All value assessment algorithms and their outputs are subject to the transparency requirements of Section 5.3

**5.3. Compensation Transparency**: All credit calculations and revenue distributions utilize transparent algorithms with public audit trails.

**5.4. No Compensation Obligation**: Contributors using only Apache 2.0 features have no compensation obligations or expectations. Compensation is purely an ecosystem enhancement benefit.

   - Granular opt-in consent for each data category

**6.3. Data Categories and Usage** (ecosystem services and structural participation):

   *Observational Data (Ecosystem Services):*
   - **LLM Interaction Data**: Prompts, responses, and context for AI training datasets
   - **Semantic Code Data**: Enhanced representations for development tool improvement
   - **Usage Analytics**: Anonymized patterns for ecosystem optimization
   - **Structured Specifications**: Curated documentation for knowledge datasets

   *Causal Data (Structural Participation):*
   - **Causal Structure Data**: Human-specified causal graphs, structural equations, edge directionality, functional forms, and compressed programs contributed through interactive training and ontology development workflows
   - **Interventional and Counterfactual Data**: Training examples generated through targeted experiments that demonstrate the effects of specific changes (interventional data) or explore alternative outcomes under modified conditions (counterfactual data), including human validations of model predictions against domain understanding

   Observational Data flows primarily through trace collection and semantic representation layers. Causal Data flows primarily through causal distillation and value assessment layers. Each data category is subject to its own consent requirements as specified in Section 6.2, with Causal Data requiring structural participation consent in addition to standard ecosystem consent.

**6.4. Data Ownership and Rights**:
   - Users retain full ownership of their original data
   - Ecosystem gains limited rights to curate, enhance, and sell derivative datasets
   - Users receive compensation for valuable data contributions
   - Deletion rights honored within technical and legal constraints

3. Optionally engage in structural participation for causal knowledge co-creation (Tier 3)

### For Commercial Users
1. Comply with attribution and value-weighted revenue sharing requirements
2. Obtain appropriate consents for data usage, distinguishing observational and causal data
